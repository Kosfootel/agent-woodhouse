# HockeyOps.ai Youth Biometric Booth Experience — Research Report

## Executive Summary

This report details equipment, costs, timing, and booth design options for a youth hockey biometric testing experience targeting kids ages 8–14. It covers stationary testing equipment, wearable sensor costs at scale, booth throughput modeling, and reference companies doing similar work. Three budget tiers are provided: **Budget** (~$3K–$6K equipment), **Mid** (~$10K–$20K), and **Premium** (~$30K–$60K+).

### Sample Booth Configurations & Throughput

#### Configuration A: Pure Camera/AI Approach (No Physical Stations)
- **What:** 2–3 cameras + AI software (pose estimation, jump analysis, sprint timing)
- **Stations:** Check-in → AI Movement Capture → Results Kiosk
- **Time per player:** ~8–10 min
- **Throughput:** ~30–45 players/hour
- **Cost:** ~$5K–$15K (cameras + software + tablet)
- **Pros:** Fast setup, no calibration between athletes, futuristic feel
- **Cons:** Less tactile/engaging, accuracy depends on camera quality, hard to compare to "real" combine numbers

#### Configuration B: Budget Physical Booth (3 Stations)
- **Stations:** Check-in → Vertical Jump (Just Jump) → 10m Sprint (Dashr) → Grip Strength → Results
- **Staffing:** 1 person at check-in, 1–2 floating between stations
- **Time per player:** ~12–15 min
- **Throughput:** ~20–30 players/hour
- **Equipment cost:** ~$3,000–$5,000
- **Pros:** Affordable, tangible "combine" feel, instant scores kids can brag about
- **Cons:** Slower throughput, fewer metrics, less "wow"

#### Configuration C: Mid-Tier Mixed Booth (4 Stations + Camera)
- **Stations:** Check-in → Vertical Jump (Skyhook) → 10m Sprint (Dashr 4-gate) → BlazePod Agility → Grip + Broad Jump → Photo/Video Station → Results
- **Staffing:** 2–3 staff + 1 photographer
- **Time per player:** ~18–22 min
- **Throughput:** ~15–20 players/hour
- **Equipment cost:** ~$12,000–$20,000
- **Pros:** Best balance of engagement and data. Shareable photo/video. Multiple "wow" moments.
- **Cons:** Requires more space, more staff, higher cost

#### Configuration D: Premium Full Experience (6+ Stations)
- **Stations:** Check-in → Force Plate (KINVENT/VALD) → Freelap Sprint (multi-split) → FitLight/Witty SEM Agility → Y-Balance → Grip + Broad Jump → Stick Velocity (Tendo/Output) → 360° Photo Booth → Results
- **Staffing:** 4–5 staff
- **Time per player:** ~30–40 min
- **Throughput:** ~8–12 players/hour
- **Equipment cost:** ~$35,000–$60,000+
- **Pros:** Pro-grade data, incredible shareability, "NHL combine" feel
- **Cons:** Expensive, slow throughput, needs large space

### Camera/AI vs Physical Stations: Comparison

| Factor | Pure Camera/AI | Mixed Physical + Camera | Full Physical |
|--------|---------------|------------------------|---------------|
| **Setup time** | Fast (hours) | Medium (half day) | Slow (full day) |
| **Throughput** | High (30–45/hr) | Medium (15–20/hr) | Low (8–12/hr) |
| **Engagement** | Medium | High | Very High |
| **Data accuracy** | Moderate (AI-dependent) | High | Very High |
| **Shareability** | High (auto-generated) | Very High ( staged + auto) | Medium (manual) |
| **Cost** | $5K–$15K | $12K–$20K | $35K–$60K+ |
| **Staff needed** | 1–2 | 3–4 | 5–6 |
| **Calibration** | Minimal | Between sessions | Per-athlete on force plates |
| **Replicability** | High (same AI model) | High | Very High |
| **"Combine feel"** | Low | High | Very High |

**Recommendation:** For youth hockey, **mixed physical + camera** is optimal. Kids need to *feel* like they're at a combine. Physical stations provide tactile engagement and instant, understandable scores. A single photo/video station with slow-mo or 360° capture gives the shareable moment that drives social media and parent satisfaction.

## 6. Key Takeaways & Recommendations

### Optimal Booth Setup for HockeyOps.ai

For a **youth hockey biometric booth** targeting kids 8–14, the recommended starting configuration is:

**Start with Tier 2 (Mid) — ~$12,000–$15,000 initial investment:**

1. **Vertical Jump Station** — Skyhook Contact Mat ($1,389). Instant jump height + reactive strength index. App shows progress.
2. **Speed Station** — Dashr Silver 4-gate ($1,300). 10m + 30m splits. Kids get "combine time."
3. **Agility Station** — BlazePod 8-pod kit (~$1,000). Reactive light race. Most fun station. Kids race each other.
4. **Strength Station** — Jamar Grip ($250) + broad jump tape ($20). Two quick tests, one station.
5. **Photo/Video Station** — Ring light + camera + branded backdrop (~$1,500). Slow-mo jump or sprint crossing. Shareable.
6. **Results Kiosk** — 2× iPads with custom leaderboard app. QR code sends report to parents.

**Add a camera/AI layer on top:**
- One high-speed camera (GoPro Hero 12 or similar, ~$400) at the jump station for slow-mo replay.
- Basic pose estimation (OpenPose or MediaPipe) running on a laptop to overlay jump mechanics. Low cost (~$0 software), high perceived value.

### Why This Wins

- **Engagement:** Kids touch equipment, compete on light races, and get instant numbers.
- **Shareability:** Photo/video station creates organic marketing.
- **Data quality:** Good enough to identify talent and track progress.
- **Throughput:** ~15–20 players/hour = 120–160 players in an 8-hour tournament day.
- **Revenue:** At $50–$75 per player, gross $6,000–$12,000 per event.
- **Payback:** 2–3 events to break even on equipment.

### Scale Path

- **Phase 1:** Run 3–5 events with Tier 2 setup. Refine workflow, gather testimonials.
- **Phase 2:** Add force plates (KINVENT or VALD) and stick velocity (Output Sports) for "Elite" tier at $100+ per player.
- **Phase 3:** Franchise the booth model to other rinks/tournaments. White-label the software platform.
